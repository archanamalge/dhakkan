    • About

    Maharashtra represents a "great nation" and is the home of Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The place where the echoes of past glory can still be heard and where the fantastic past and rich civilization of the Marathas can be seen. Many empires constructed architectural wonders that have endured to tell the story of ancient eras. Maharashtra has nearly 350 forts, drawing numerous architecture enthusiasts across the country. The British systematically destroyed many forts during the conquest and burned down all crucial forts in the Deccan in 1818. Many of these forts have become excellent rock climbing, trekking, and camping destinations. Maharashtra fort treks is a Treks and Trails project that connects adventurers to the untamed nature and gives them a platform to develop their adventurous side while breathing fresh air.

    Raigad Fort

    Raigad Fort, located in Maharashtra, India, is a historic hill fortress that holds immense significance in Indian history. It was the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj's Maratha Empire and served as the seat of his coronation in 1674.

    Shivneri Fort

    Shivneri Fort, situated near Junnar in Maharashtra, India, is renowned as the birthplace of the legendary Maratha warrior king, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. This historic fort is perched atop a hill and boasts impressive architectural features, including robust ramparts, gates, and water cisterns.

    Explore
    Sindhudurga Fort

    Sindhudurg Fort, located in the Arabian Sea off the coast of Malvan in Maharashtra, India, is a historic sea fort built by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Constructed in the 17th century, this fortress is known for its robust architecture and strategic location. The fort is surrounded by the sea on all sides and features intricate carvings, bastions, and a hidden passage.

    Torna Fort

    Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a historic hill fort located near Pune in Maharashtra, India. It holds the distinction of being the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1646, marking the beginning of the Maratha Empire's expansion. Torna Fort is perched at an altitude of about 1,403 meters and offers panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain range.

    Harishchandragad Fort

    Harishchandragad Fort, located in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, India, is a historic hill fort known for its scenic beauty and ancient temples. Situated in the Western Ghats, the fort offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and peaks.

    Korigad Fort

    Korigad fort trek is an easy level trek located about 20 km from Lonavla in Pune district. The topmost point on the trek is about 3028 ft above sea level. Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the great Maratha ruler incorporated Korigad, Lohagad, Visapur, Tung and Tikona in 1657. It is mentioned that this fort was taken over by the Pant Secretary in the year 1700. The Peshwas later captured it and was used for government detention.

    Purandar Fort

    Purandar Fort also called Purandhar Fort signifies the rise of Shivaji against Adil Shahi Bijapur Sultanate and the Mughals. It is among one of the famous tourist attraction in Pune, Maharashtra, India. The fort is situated in the Western Ghats and stands 4472 ft above sea level. Purandar and Vajragad or Rudramal fort are twin forts.
